Physical formats

One thing I've gotten very focused on recently is building out how to consume media using only physical formats, completley removing streaming services from my life. And I've mostly figured out what formats I'm going to utilize for my physical formats. For video, HD-DVD, Blu-ray, and DVD will be used (priortiy highest to lowest) and for Music it's Vinyl. I do think that owning the physical versions of these things are better than just streaming it online. I feel more connected to what I'm watching, and the bonus content that comes with physical things just makes it so much better.

HD-DVD

HD-DVD was a format developed by Toshiba in 2002 released 2006 and discontinued in 2008. This format was the competitor for the HD video format against Blu-ray, which ended up winning the video format war after Sony's bold play with the PlayStation 3 having a Blu-ray player built in. Now, why does this neice abandoned video format have its own section? Because that's one of my hyperfixations silly! Yeah, for some reason my autism gravitated towards this format just because of the "cool factor" of the HD-DVD name and other things about it.
